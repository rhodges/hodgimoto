Date: November 28, 2014, 8:10 PM PST

Mars? No: Process!

I like building things. Find a problem, engineer a solution, execute the plan, then go hunt for new problems. It's great, it's fun, and it sounds like a good plan, unless you're actually an engineer and you see what's wrong with this picture: no documentation (or iteration, reflection, or several other things that should be included in any solid plan). This site is intended to do several things, not only to give me a playground where I can show off some of my favorite solutions, but also to force me to be more disciplined in my approach and record how and why I did everything so that I don't have to ask these questions over and over again.

For example: today is the day after thanksgiving, and to help get me away from family and on the road home at a decent hour, I dangled a carrot in front of myself:

Daydream vision:

Tonight I will make a sweet-bunned map of Mars with a wicked layer switcher, and some cool cartography and I'll be the greatest person on the internet and NASA will try to hire me and I'll say, modestly, "thanks, but I can't desert my current employer, for their work is mighty and they need me more. But maybe we can work out a deal where you can pay them to pay me to solve the world's problems with code-science." I will learn OL3 and build it in that, maybe with an option to bring in some 3D effects since spinny-globes are cooler than mercator! The world is lucky to have me. I think of everything.

PS: D3 SPINNY GLOBES!

Of course, as I cursed at Black Friday traffic wanting to drive 80mph through cars going 3mph due to a bad storm and an unfortunate accident, I had some time to pick this "clearly flawless" plan apart.

  • My site is just a Yeoman generated skeleton
  • Despite years of OL2 experience, I don't know how to use OL3
  • Can I even get a basemap for Mars?
  • Can I even get a projection of Mars that will work for web maps?
  • Will I have time to also finish laundry and scoop the cat litter?
  • If I build this, will I learn anything from it?

So, I decided instead to start filling in my blog page so I'd have a place to share my lessons as the 2 hour project in my head inevitably manifested itself into the 16+ hour White Rabbit-Hydra hybrid monster that I'm almost guaranteed to turn it into (for example, since when is step 1 of making a map "start a blog"?).

Also, note my willingness to assume technical debt already: I could install Django on my server, tie my AngularJS site to it, drop in a promising looking blog module and have this problem solved for good, but that brings in two very discouraging complications:

  1. This makes it even less likely that I will even start the map-making process tonight.
  2. People will be able to respond, make fun of me, and point out examples of people having done this before.

Granted, #2 is not a concern. This site is not advertised, anticipated, renowned, or even close to being worth reading. That being said, I'm now aware that every painful sentence I drag out for this first entry is not helping at all.

Objective 1: Get concise.
